Become a Speaker

DIA invites motivated researchers, graduate students, and academic practitioners to contribute to an open intercollegiate space for rigorous intellectual exchange.

The Duby Intercollegiate Associateship was founded to facilitate the circulation of high-quality academic knowledge beyond traditional institutional boundaries. Central to this mission is the contribution of speakers willing to share their research, insights, and intellectual perspectives with a motivated international student audience.

DIA lectures typically feature advanced undergraduate researchers, graduate students, doctoral candidates, and early-career academics from leading universities. Presentations are conducted online in a structured yet accessible format designed to encourage clarity, rigor, and meaningful discussion.

Participation as a speaker is entirely voluntary and non-commercial. The programme operates on principles of scientific solidarity, intellectual curiosity, and academic openness.

What we look for

Original Research

Speakers should present structured academic work grounded in serious research and clear analytical argument.

Clarity & Pedagogy

Lectures should remain accessible to a motivated student audience while preserving academic rigor.

Institutional Diversity

DIA particularly values contributions from a wide range of universities and academic environments.

Intellectual Seriousness

The programme prioritises depth of thought, methodological care, and genuine scholarly engagement.
