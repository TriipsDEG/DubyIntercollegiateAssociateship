The DIA Programme is a structured intercollegiate academic initiative organised around a semester-long cycle of ten carefully curated lectures, divided into two thematic parts and separated by a short mid-term break. This format ensures both intellectual coherence and sustained engagement across institutions.

Each lecture features at least one invited speaker drawn from leading universities, graduate programmes, and professional institutions. Sessions are designed to make rigorous, high-level academic and scientific research publicly accessible to a broad student audience. All lectures are delivered online, recorded, and made openly available on the DIA website and YouTube platform, ensuring flexible, inclusive, and intellectually democratic participation.

To encourage active engagement, participants may complete optional short quizzes throughout the semester. Successful completion of at least seven out of ten quizzes leads to the award of a formal DIA certificate, recognising sustained intellectual commitment. Students may also submit an independent dissertation, contributing original research aligned with the programme’s thematic focus and competing for the annual dissertation distinction.

Practical Format
