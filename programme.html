The DIA Programme is a structured intercollegiate academic initiative built around a semester-long cycle of ten carefully curated lectures organised into two thematic parts separated by a short mid-term break.

Each lecture features at least one invited speaker and is designed to make advanced academic and scientific knowledge accessible to a broad student audience across institutions. All sessions are recorded and made openly available on the DIA website and YouTube platform, ensuring flexible and inclusive participation.

To encourage active engagement, participants may complete optional short quizzes throughout the semester; successful completion leads to the award of a formal DIA certificate. Students may also submit an independent dissertation, with outstanding work recognised through the annual DIA Dissertation Prize.

Practical Format

Lectures are delivered online via Google Meet. Each session features at least one invited speaker drawn from leading universities, graduate programmes, and professional institutions.
